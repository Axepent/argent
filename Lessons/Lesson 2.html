Artificial Languages in Media and Games

People are interesting creatures, we love to create — and not just stories or paintings, but languages too. These are called artificial languages (or constructed languages, conlangs for short).

An artificial language is a language that did not evolve (or even appear) naturally, like English or Chinese, but was intentionally created by a person or group of people. These languages have their own grammar, vocabulary, and sometimes even writing systems.

Some artificial languages are made for fun, others for art, and some even for serious communication.

We’ll look at a few fascinating examples that were created for storytelling and gaming:

  • Elvish Language (Quenya & Sindarin) – from The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien
  • Enchanting Table Language – the cryptic runes seen in Minecraft
  • Glyph Language – from the mysterious indie game Tunic

These are just a few examples that we’ll look at more closely. But artificial languages appear all over creative media, enriching stories and drawing players and readers deeper into fictional worlds. Here are a few more worth mentioning:

  • Esperanto
    Created in the late 19th century by L. L. Zamenhof, Esperanto is an artificial language meant for international communication. It was designed to be easy to learn and politically neutral — and it still has a small but passionate global community today.
  • Old Entish (Tolkien)
    Another of Tolkien’s inventions, Entish is the ancient, slow language of the tree-like Ents. It reflects their thoughtful and deliberate nature — so slow to speak that most beings simply don’t have the patience to use it.
  • NieR: Automata
    The game features a constructed vocal language used in its music, designed to sound like a blend of many real-world languages. It gives the soundtrack a haunting, timeless quality that fits the game's post-apocalyptic world.
  • Nowai Language (Outer Wilds)
    In Outer Wilds, players encounter messages written in an alien script left by an ancient race. While the game translates the words for the player, the script and its spiral structure reflect the alien culture’s way of thinking and learning.
  • Languages in Chants of Sennaar
    This puzzle game revolves entirely around deciphering several unknown written languages. Each one has its own logic and symbols, and players must learn to read and translate them as part of the core gameplay.
  • Final Fantasy Series
    Across its many titles, Final Fantasy has introduced several fictional languages — including the Al Bhed language in Final Fantasy X, which players can gradually learn and translate. These languages add richness to the fantasy settings and cultures.

These languages aren't just decoration — they help build worlds, shape cultures, and bring something entirely new into existence, whether in a fictional universe or the real one.

Humanity has created many fascinating inventions, but language might be the most important of all. Even though we still don’t have a single universal language, it remains one of the few things that truly connects us.
